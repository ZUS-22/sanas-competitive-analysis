Product: Accent Translation

Build ~17% Captured — $101M of $595M
What it does: Converts recorded audio from a source accent (Indian, Filipino, LATAM English) to a target accent (US English, UK English) while preserving the speaker's words, emotion, and voice identity. Input: audio file. Output: accent-modified audio file.

Executive Decision: BUILD — Highest Priority

The accent conversion space is structurally unserved. Krisp, Tomato.ai, and AccentHarmonizer are all streaming-only. Resemble AI and ElevenLabs have APIs but neither does true accent conversion (Resemble re-synthesizes via voice cloning; ElevenLabs changes voice style, not accent). Sanas is the only player with production-proven, identity-preserving accent translation at scale (30K+ agents). The first-mover window is open but closing — ElevenLabs and foundation model providers (OpenAI, Google) could commoditize this capability within 12–18 months.

Opportunity Score: 11.0 / 20 — Genuine Unmet Job

G2 Review Evidence: Users Want Accent Conversion, Not Voice Cloning

Users of ElevenLabs, Resemble AI, and Respeecher are attempting accent conversion through voice cloning — but voice cloning architecturally couples accent + identity. The reviews reveal a consistent unmet need: change the accent, keep the voice identity.

Signal Patterns from Reviews

Signal PatternWhat It ProvesEvidence from Reviews
Accent Bleed
Mixed accents in output		 Users want clean accent output but get uncontrollable mixing — the tool can’t isolate accent from identity ElevenLabs G2: “When changing voiceover to a different accent, it doesn’t really dilute the previous/base accent, resulting in a mixture of accents (like Indian plus Latin). If I’m opting for British English, I’d rather it be entirely British.”

Resemble AI: Cross-language transfer requires setting cfg_weight=0.0 to “reduce accent bleed” — an undocumented API workaround.
Accent Drift / Switching
Accent changes mid-generation		 Users need consistent accent across long-form content but the model can’t hold it ElevenLabs: “The AI randomly switches accents or languages mid-sentence in longer content. A 10-minute audio might start American English and end up British or even slip into other languages entirely.”

Beyond 800–900 characters, accent consistency degrades noticeably across passages.
Accent Not Captured
Target accent distorted or flattened		 Users want to target a specific accent but cloning flattens or distorts it Respeecher G2: “British and Indian accents were not even close to authentic.”

ElevenLabs GitHub #649: Dutch voices produce strong English accents despite correct language selection — a model-level architectural problem, not configuration error.

Product-by-Product Accent Gaps

ElevenLabs — 1,140+ G2 reviews, “accent” mentioned in 715 reviews
  • No direct accent parameter. Users cannot adjust accent without Professional Voice Cloning ($99+/mo). Voice Remixing adjusts delivery/cadence only, not accent.
  • English phonetic bias: Models are “primarily trained on large datasets with a strong English phonetic bias”, causing English pronunciation to override target language rules.
  • Indian/Hinglish accents must be “manually fine-tuned” — no out-of-box support for the exact accents Sanas targets.
  • Long-form degradation: Beyond 800–900 characters, accent consistency degrades.
  • Workaround: “Find a voice that already has the accent you want” — users can’t change the accent of their own voice.
Resemble AI — G2 reviews
  • Accent preservation, not conversion. Resemble preserves the source accent — it does not convert accents. No parameter to say “convert Indian English → US English.”
  • Cross-language accent bleed: Requires undocumented cfg_weight=0.0 API parameter to reduce bleed.
  • Regional dialect gap: “Replicating regional accents or niche dialects can be challenging, which may affect global applications.”
  • Architecture: You clone a voice; the accent comes with it. No accent-only control exists.
Respeecher — G2 reviews + product docs
  • Closest to accent conversion but limited. Offers 3 modes: “Yours” (keep original), “Balanced” (mix), and target speaker’s accent. 12 English accents.
  • Quality gap: G2 reviewers noted “British and Indian accents were not even close” to authentic.
  • English training bias: “Since the system is mainly trained on English, it may add a slight English accent to conversions.”
  • Self-described early stage: Respeecher states accent conversion is “in quite early stages, though samples are quite promising.”
  • Price barrier: $5.40/min makes processing prohibitively expensive.

Product: Noise Cancellation

Bundle Only ~27% Captured — $130M of $483M
What it does: Removes background noise, echo, and reverb from recorded audio files. Input: noisy audio file. Output: clean audio file. No transcription, no accent change.

Executive Decision: ALREADY INCLUDED — Part of Accent Translation

Noise Cancellation is already built into the Accent Translation product. Dolby.io Media Enhance is the market leader but has meaningful satisfaction gaps (pricing, support, developer experience). Open-source alternatives (RNNoise, DeepFilterNet) are widespread. Standalone NC is not differentiated enough for Sanas and does not need a separate product — AT already delivers NC as part of its processing pipeline. No additional investment required.

Product: ASR — Automatic Speech Recognition

Partner ~45% Captured — $644M of $1.43B
What it does: Converts recorded audio files into text transcripts at scale. Input: audio file. Output: text transcript. No audio modification.

Executive Decision: PARTNER — Do Not Build Standalone

The ASR market is commoditized. Whisper is free and self-hostable. Deepgram and AssemblyAI are well-funded, developer-first, and racing to the bottom on pricing ($0.003–$0.006/min). Sanas has no ASR IP today. The only viable path is a partnership model: Sanas accent-aware preprocessing + Deepgram/AssemblyAI transcription, exposed as a single combined API endpoint. This is a product strategy decision, not a build decision. Sanas's unique value is improving WER on accented audio — not competing on transcription itself.

Opportunity Score: 11.0 / 20 — Genuine demand, but highly competitive

Sanas Revenue Model: Partnership Revenue Share

Sanas does not build ASR. Revenue comes from two streams: (1) Preprocessing API: $0.05–$0.08/min for NC+AT preprocessing before partner ASR — Sanas keeps 100%. (2) Combined endpoint: Sanas preprocessing + Deepgram/AssemblyAI transcription sold as a single API at $0.054–$0.084/min — Sanas keeps ~85% ($0.05–$0.08), partner gets ~15% ($0.004/min pass-through). Potential: If 10% of Sanas's existing BPO customers route recorded calls through the combined endpoint = ~$1.5M ARR preprocessing revenue, growing with AT adoption.

Why Sanas should partner, not build
1. Price floor is $0. Whisper is open-source and free. No pricing premium is sustainable for standalone ASR.
2. Feature parity is table stakes. Market expects diarization, PII redaction, summarization, sentiment — building all this takes years.
3. Sanas's unique value is audio preprocessing. Accent translation + noise cancellation before ASR measurably improves WER on accented speech. This is a complement, not a substitute.
4. Partnership math works. Sanas preprocessing ($0.05–$0.08/min) + Deepgram transcription ($0.004/min) = combined $0.054–$0.084/min endpoint that delivers best-in-class accented speech accuracy.

Product: Speech Enhancement

Bundle ~20% Captured — $120M of $600M
What it does: Comprehensive audio quality improvement beyond noise removal. Solves: (1) Reverb/echo removal — eliminates room reflections and hollow sound. (2) Microphone upscaling — transforms low-quality laptop/phone mic recordings to studio-grade clarity. (3) Energy normalization — boosts low-energy, mumbled speech to clear, high-energy output with consistent loudness. (4) Network audio repair — fixes compression artifacts, packet loss distortion, and bandwidth-limited audio from VoIP/video calls. Input: degraded audio file. Output: studio-quality enhanced audio file.

Executive Decision: BUILD LIGHT — Bundle into AT Pipeline

The standalone speech enhancement market is crowded: Adobe Enhance Speech is free and excellent, Dolby.io owns the API space, and Auphonic dominates podcast post-production. Building a standalone product is not viable. However, speech enhancement is a critical preprocessing step that directly improves Accent Translation output quality. Since AT already includes Noise Cancellation, bundling reverb removal, mic upscaling, and energy normalization into the AT pipeline (Enhancement → AT) creates a differentiated end-to-end audio processing pipeline that no competitor offers. The AI-powered audio enhancer market is $1.26B (2025), growing at 33.4% CAGR — the opportunity is real, but only as part of a combined offering.
